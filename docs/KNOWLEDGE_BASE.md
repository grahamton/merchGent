# Unified B2B Commerce Standard

## UX & Interface Standard (Authority: Baymard Institute)

- **Principle**: Minimize checkout friction.
- **Guideline**: Guest checkout should be available for B2C; "Quick Order" pads are essential for B2B.
- **Guideline**: Search results must support faceted navigation for high-SKU catalogs.

## Strategy & Model Standard (Authority: Forrester Research)

- **Principle**: Differentiate "Shoppers" (B2C) from "Buyers" (B2B).
- **Shoppers**: Seek discovery, deals, and emotional connection.
- **Buyers**: Seek efficiency, recurring orders, and contract compliance.

## Procurement Standard (Authority: CIPS)

- **Principle**: Compliance is key.
- **Definition**: **Maverick Spend** is buying outside approved contracts. B2B sites must make it easier to buy _on-contract_ than off.
- **Tools**: Punch-out catalogs (OCI, cXML) are the gold standard for enterprise integration.

## Data Standard (Authority: GS1)

- **Principle**: Unambiguous Identification.
- **Guideline**: GTINs (Global Trade Item Numbers) should be visible and indexable.
- **Guideline**: Attributes must be normalized (e.g., "mm" not "millimeters" in specs).

# Professional B2B Procurement Glossary

- **cXML (Commerce eXtensible Markup Language)**: A standard protocol for B2B procurement data exchange, often used for punch-out catalogs.
- **OCI (Open Catalog Interface)**: An SAP standard for connecting external catalogs to procurement systems.
- **Punch-Out**: A mechanism allowing a buyer to access a supplier's website from within their own procurement application.
- **Maverick Spend**: Unmanaged, ad-hoc spending that bypasses established procurement policies, often reducing profitability.
- **SKU Proliferation**: The excessive growth of product variations, leading to inventory management issues.
